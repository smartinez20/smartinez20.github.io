KILLER WHALE

They are the largest members of the dolphin family, and they hunt: fish, squids, seals, sea lions, sea birds, etc.

BOTTLENOSE

Bottlenose dolphins live in groups, and they live in warm oceans and tropical seas.

AMAZON RIVER DOLPHIN

They are the largest species or river dolphins, and the adults, mostly the males, acquire a pink color.

BAIJI

They are an extinct species of freshwater dolphis that were only found in the Yangtze River in China.

INDO-PACIFIC HUMPBACKS

The Indo-Pacific Humpbacks undergo dramatic growth until they reach the age of 10 and then grow at slow rates until the age of 25. Their average length is 250 cm.

SHORT-BEAK

They are a species of common dolphin. They can be found in the Caribbean and Mediterrainean seas, as well as in the Pacific and Atlantic oceans.

STRIPPED

They are part of the oceanic dolphin family and can be found in all of the temperate and tropical parts of the world's oceans.

TUCUXI

The Tucuxi is a freshwater dolphin that can be found in the rivers of the Amazon Basin.

FALSE KILLER WHALE

The False Killer Whale is the third largest dolphin, and is an oceanic dolphin. They live in temperate and tropical waters throughout the world.

RISSO'S

They are commonly known as the Monk dolphin. They're named after Antoine Risso, who formed the first public description of the dolphin.

SPINNER

They are small dolphins that are found in off-shore tropical waters, and theyare famous for their acrobatic displays.

PANTROPICAL

They are found in tropical oceans, and were once under threat because many died during the tuna purse seines.
